Hot wallets

Hot wallets also known as web wallets are crypto currency wallets that store crypto and are constantly connected to the internet via a user interface, a bit like email account is constantly connected to the web. These types of wallets are very useful in terms of convenience especially if you are a trader, as access is pretty instant in terms of just your password in most cases is needed and you are good to spend and send your crypto as you wish. as opposed to cold storage wallets that are a lot more time consuming to access your funds, although this is the whole point and part of the security feature to cryptos stored in a cold wallet. Below are some of the most trusted and commonly used hot wallets to-date. When using these types of wallets, because they are constantly connected to the internet unlike cold wallets are, it is important to follow as best procedures as posibble in order to stay safe from potential hackers and phishing. such as procedures below is a good idea to follow:

  • Use secure internet
  • Use a VPN
  • Secure your devices(admin/password locks)
  • Keep passwords/seed phrases in a safe place
  • Always be sure to check the correct spelling of any wallet or website in your browser to combat phising attacks

If you do your part and stick to the physical security features noted above, hot wallets are really great tools have and use, bare in mind probably not wise to keep a large amount of your funds on them just for the added security by yourself. For example, lets say you had $10,000 in crypto. If you have this amount purely to store away as an investment, it would be wise to use a cold wallet. If you had $10,000 in crypto and you are a trader, you might keep may be $3000 at time to trade with and leave the rest on a cold wallet. This will differ from person to person and the amounts and frequencies that you are playing with but you get the gist. Below are some of the most frequently used hot wallets.

  • Exodus
    Atomic
    Metamask
    TrustWallet
Note: many blockchain projects also have their own designated wallets for their native token.

These types of wallets on the other hand do come with great features these days such as exchanging one crypto currency for another within the wallet which allows for even greater secuurity as you do not have to send your funds to a central exchangeable in order to exchange from one crypto currenct to the next, particularly useful if you are new to the crypto world and do not want to get to involved in the technicalities of moving your funds around. Another feature is a many hot wallets also now now participate and provide staking features within them which allows for light defi and interest bearing exposure. There are usually charts and coin metrics that you can gather information from also.

Cold wallets

Cold wallets also known as hardware wallets are the best form of securing your funds that you can have as unlike hot wallets, your wallet and funds are stored off-line due to the private keys being stored on the actual hardware device as opposed to hot wallets where the private keys are online(stored on the web wallet). The whole seed phrase and password situation has been to be treated just as crucial as with hot wallets because these are access to your funds as like your banking information only that there is absolutely no chance of mal practice due to the off-line nature of your crypto being stored.
There are a number of various cold/hardware wallets on the market that all have a good reputation for security and useability. Below are the main actors in the market today:

  • Ledger Nano
  • Trezor
  • D'cent
  • Ellipal
The Ledger nano and Trezor are the two earlier wallets that have been around for the last five + years, the other two are newer versions inside the last three years but all are credible devices to date. all with the same common goal in mind. They all have their unique features and bluetooth capabilities. Do some reserch.
